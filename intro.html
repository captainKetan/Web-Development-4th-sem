Semi-Conductor Laboratory (SCL) strives on the policy to manage its activities in a responsible manner as to avoid causing any risks to the health and safety of its personnel. With the technology upgradation and rapid expansion of newer processes, self-help in minimising the occurrence/devastating effects of accidents has become essential for all industries.

Wafer processing and its allied operations at SCL require handling of large number of hazardous gases and chemicals. Various gases/ chemicals are pyrophoric, toxic, corrosive or flammable in nature. To ensure incident free operations at SCL, the installed safety systems and their up-keep thus is of great importance. Different safety installations, comprising of fire protection and detection system, Toxic Gas Monitoring (TGM) system, Chemical Leak Detection system, etc. are maintained for 100% up-time on 24x7 basis.

Fire & Safety Section (FSS) is entrusted with the responsibilities to ensure overall safety of operations at SCL. Main functions of FSS involves:
  • Up-keep of various safety installations viz. Fire Hydrant and Sprinkler System, Fire Detection system, Toxic Gas Monitoring system, Chemical Leak Detection system etc.
  • Attending emergency calls.
  • Planning for ERT functions.
  • Conducting safety mock drill.
  • Conducting safety training programmes for SCL staff/ contractor's labour at site.
Following life safety systems are installed within the campus:
  • Fire Suppression System - Yard Hydrant & Wet Riser, High Velocity Water spray, Automatic Sprinkler system.
  • Toxic Gas Detection System to monitor all strategic location for monitoring accidental release, if any, of hazardous process gases in ppb levels.
  • Addressable Analog Fire Alarm System and Repeater Panels interfaced with Gas Detection System.
  • All Fire Alarm System integrated with Public Address System.
  • First Aid Fire Fighting Equipment - Hose Reel, Fire Extinguishers.
  • Safety Eye & Body Showers.
  • Portable Air Breathing Unit are deployed at all strategic locations.
  • Also, in-house Fire Station is manned on 24x7 basis by trained personnel of CISF fire wing. The station is equipped with:
    • Fire Tender
    • Portable Fire Pump

Class 4:
An incident that causes multiple fatalities ( more than one ), space mishaps occurring during space vehicle operations, and/or damage to equipment or property equal to or greater than 1.0 crore or if the failure/damage is likely to have significant impact on the programme.

Class 3:
An incident that causes a death and/or damage to equipment or property equal to or greater than 20 lakhs but less than 1.0 crore or if the failure/damage is likely to have significant delay in the project schedule.

Class 2:
An incident that results in permanent total disability of one or more persons; or hospitalization (for other than observation) of three or more persons; and/or damage to equipment or property equal to or greater than 10 lakhs but less than 20 lakhs.

Class 1:
An incident that results in permanent partial disability or Lost Time Accidents (LTA) greater than 48 hours; and/or damage to equipment or property equal to or greater than 2.0 lakhs but less than 10 lakhs.

Class 0:
An incident that results in temporary total disability or Lost Time Accidents (LTA) less than 48 hours; and/or damage to equipment or property less than 2.0 lakhs.

Near Miss:
An occurrence in which there is no injury or minor injury requiring first-aid, no significant equipment property damage (less than 1.0 lakh), and no significant interruption of productive work, butpossesses a high potential to cause an accident.

All chemicals/gases shall be used with due care as per the recommended handling/storage procedures and with proper PPE (Personal Protection Equipment) to avoid exposure to toxic chemicals/gases. In case of accidental exposure to any chemical/gas the affected person(s) shall be given first aid, immediately and rushed to the nearest hospital/dispensary for further treatment, as may be required. While medical staff typically treats such patients symptomatically, a compilation of the suggested antidotes/treatment specific to the chemical/gas exposure is made in consultation with medical experts from All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), New Delhi.
